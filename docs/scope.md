# EAIOS 1 Scope

## Mission

Demonstrate that EAIOS can orchestrate enterprise reasoning to achieve a desired business outcome through capability-based execution, explainable reasoning, governed decision-making, and continuous enterprise learning.

EAIOS 1 validates the operating model using the **Application Health Management** use case.

---

# In Scope

## Desired Business Outcomes

Business outcomes define what the enterprise is trying to achieve.

All reasoning and execution are aligned to achieving that outcome.

Examples:

* Maintain Application Health
* Restore Service Availability
* Reduce Incident Volume

---

## Capability Assessment

Determine whether an enterprise AI capability exists to support the desired business outcome.

Possible outcomes:

* Capability Exists
* Capability Does Not Exist

Capability gaps are identified and recorded for future evolution.

---

## Capability-Based Execution

Capabilities represent reusable enterprise functionality used to achieve business outcomes.

Example:

* Application Health Management

---

## Task Planning

Capabilities are decomposed into executable tasks.

Example:

* Collect Telemetry
* Analyze Logs
* Search Knowledge
* Perform Reasoning
* Recommend Action

---

## Skill Matching

Tasks are translated into the enterprise skills required to achieve the desired outcome.

Examples:

* Log Analysis
* Knowledge Retrieval
* KT Problem Analysis
* Risk Assessment

---

## Implementation Selection

The most appropriate implementation of each required skill is selected and orchestrated.

Implementations may include:

* AI Agents
* Enterprise Services
* Workflows
* Future execution mechanisms

---

## Enterprise Memory

Enterprise Memory provides historical patterns that support enterprise reasoning.

It stores validated patterns rather than individual execution events.

Enterprise Memory contributes to:

* Pattern recognition
* Historical context
* Operational Confidence
* Enterprise learning

---

## Operational Confidence

Operational Confidence determines how much enterprise reasoning is required before action is taken.

Operational Confidence influences:

* Execution Strategy
* Human validation requirements
* Knowledge retrieval depth

---

## Strategy Selection

Execution strategy is selected based on:

* Current situation
* Enterprise Memory
* Operational Confidence

Example strategies:

* Accelerated Validation
* Standard Investigation
* Full Enterprise Research

---

## Safety and Governance

All execution must be:

* Explainable
* Governed
* Observable
* Auditable

Human approval may be required before execution.

---

## Execution Trace

Execution Trace records:

* Desired Business Outcome
* Capability
* Tasks
* Skills
* Selected Implementations
* Operational Confidence
* Execution Strategy
* Safety Decisions
* Recommendations
* Outcomes

---

## Enterprise Learning

Enterprise Learning captures organizational learning from execution.

Learning includes:

* Validated patterns
* Reasoning outcomes
* Human interventions
* Safety decisions
* Improvement opportunities

Enterprise learning belongs to the enterprise rather than individual agents.

---

Planned for EAIOS 2.

# Success Criteria

EAIOS 1 successfully demonstrates the following enterprise reasoning workflow:

```text
Desired Business Outcome
            ↓
Capability Assessment
            ↓
Task Planning
            ↓
Skill Matching
            ↓
Implementation Selection
            ↓
Enterprise Memory
            ↓
Operational Confidence
            ↓
Execution Strategy
            ↓
Recommendation
            ↓
Enterprise Learning
```

using the **Application Health Management** use case with realistic enterprise validation scenarios.

---

# Guiding Principle

EAIOS 1 is an architectural operating model.

Its purpose is not to maximize AI intelligence.

Its purpose is to demonstrate how enterprise reasoning transforms collective intelligence into explainable, governed, continuously improving decisions.
