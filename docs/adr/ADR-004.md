\# ADR-004: Business Outcome Driven Capability Orchestration



\## Status



Accepted



\---



\## Context



Enterprises operate through business outcomes rather than individual AI agents.



The purpose of EAIOS is to determine whether a governed AI capability exists that can contribute to achieving a business outcome.



If a capability exists, EAIOS orchestrates AI execution.



If a capability does not exist, execution remains within the traditional enterprise while the capability gap is recorded as an opportunity for future capability development.



EAIOS focuses on AI-enabled enterprise capabilities rather than attempting to orchestrate all enterprise resources.



\---



\## Decision



EAIOS adopts a Business Outcome Driven orchestration model.



Execution begins with a Business Outcome.



The runtime evaluates whether an AI Capability exists that supports the desired outcome.



If an AI Capability exists, EAIOS decomposes the work into Tasks, maps Tasks to Skills, maps Skills to available Agents, and orchestrates execution through governed workflows.



If no capability exists, the business outcome continues through traditional enterprise execution while the capability gap is recorded.

\---



\## Enterprise Learning



Enterprise Learning captures:



\* Observations

\* Evidence

\* Reasoning outcomes

\* Safety outcomes

\* Human interventions

\* Capability improvement opportunities



Learning is applied to improve future execution and identify new AI capability opportunities.



\---



\## Safety and Governance



Safety is a first-class concern within EAIOS.



AI execution must be:



\* Explainable

\* Governed

\* Observable

\* Auditable



Human review and approval may be required depending on capability risk and enterprise policy.



\---



\## Scope Boundary



Version 1 includes:



\* Business Outcomes

\* Capability Assessment

\* Tasks

\* Skills

\* Agent Orchestration

\* Explainable Reasoning

\* Safety and Governance

\* Enterprise Learning



Version 1 excludes:



\* AI Asset Management

\* Agent Portfolio Management

\* Agent Lifecycle Management

\* Enterprise-wide orchestration beyond AI capabilities



These concerns are reserved for future versions.



\---



\## Consequences



EAIOS becomes an Enterprise AI Operating System focused on business outcomes rather than agent execution.



Capabilities become the primary architectural abstraction.



Agents become implementations of skills rather than the center of the architecture.



The enterprise gains visibility into:



\* Supported AI capabilities

\* Capability gaps

\* Cost and value

\* Safety and governance

\* Organizational learning
