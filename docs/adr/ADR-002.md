# ADR-002: Business Outcome First Architecture

## Status

Accepted

**Date:** 2026-06-23

---

# Context

Many AI initiatives begin with technology selection, model selection, agent development, or chatbot implementation.

This often results in isolated solutions that are difficult to govern, reuse, measure, and scale across the enterprise.

Organizations do not invest in AI to build agents.

Organizations invest in AI to achieve desired business outcomes.

An enterprise operating model should therefore begin with the desired business outcome and work backwards to identify the capabilities, skills, knowledge, implementations, humans, tools, and workflows required to achieve it.

---

# Decision

EAIOS adopts a **Business Outcome First** architecture.

All enterprise capabilities must be traceable to a measurable desired business outcome.

The primary architectural decomposition is:

```text
Desired Business Outcome
        ↓
Capability
        ↓
Skill
        ↓
Implementation
```

Implementations may include:

* AI Agents
* Humans
* Enterprise Applications
* Workflows
* Automation
* External Services

Capabilities represent collections of reusable enterprise skills.

Skills represent reusable enterprise assets.

Implementations may evolve over time without changing the desired outcome, capability, or skill model.

This architectural decomposition provides the foundation for Enterprise Reasoning.

---

# Consequences

## Benefits

* Strong alignment to business value
* Improved governance and prioritization
* Greater enterprise reuse
* Reduced technology-driven architecture decisions
* Clear traceability from implementation to desired outcome
* Stable enterprise architecture despite changing technologies

---

## Trade-offs

* Requires capability and skill modeling
* Introduces additional architectural abstraction
* Requires clearly defined business outcomes and ownership
* Requires discipline in maintaining enterprise capabilities

---

# Example

## Desired Business Outcome

Maintain Application Health

```text
Desired Business Outcome
Maintain Application Health

        ↓

Capability
Application Health Management

        ↓

Skills

• Telemetry Collection
• Knowledge Retrieval
• Pattern Recognition
• Problem Analysis
• Recommendation Generation

        ↓

Implementations

• Telemetry Agent
• Knowledge Agent
• Reasoning Agent
• Recommendation Agent
• Human Operator
```

Alternative implementations could later replace or augment these without changing the enterprise capability.

---

# Architectural Principle

Desired business outcomes drive enterprise capabilities.

Capabilities are built from reusable enterprise skills.

Implementations are replaceable.

Enterprise value is measured by outcomes rather than implementations.

Enterprise Reasoning transforms these reusable capabilities into explainable, governed decisions.
