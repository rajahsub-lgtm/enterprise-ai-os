# ADR-005: Collective Intelligence as a First-Class Enterprise Capability

## Status

Accepted

**Date:** 2026-06-23

---

## Context

Traditional enterprise systems focus on managing applications, infrastructure, services, incidents, changes, and knowledge.

Many AI platforms focus on managing models, prompts, agents, and workflows.

While these assets are important, they are not the primary objective of Enterprise AI.

Organizations invest in AI to improve their ability to solve business problems.

As AI capabilities mature, organizations accumulate knowledge, experience, reasoning outcomes, successful actions, failed actions, governance decisions, and human feedback.

This accumulated intelligence represents a strategic enterprise asset.

EAIOS requires a mechanism to measure, govern, improve, and reuse this intelligence across capabilities, skills, agents, humans, tools, workflows, and business outcomes.

---

## Decision

EAIOS shall treat Collective Intelligence as a first-class enterprise capability.

Collective Intelligence represents the enterprise's accumulated ability to achieve business outcomes through the effective application of capabilities, skills, knowledge, reasoning, governance, and learning.

Collective Intelligence is not owned by any individual agent, model, application, or workflow.

Collective Intelligence belongs to the enterprise.

EAIOS shall continuously capture, measure, govern, and improve Collective Intelligence through execution and learning.

---

## Components of Collective Intelligence

Collective Intelligence may include:

* Enterprise Knowledge
* Historical Outcomes
* Reasoning Results
* Known Errors
* Incident History
* Successful Resolutions
* Failed Resolutions
* Human Feedback
* Governance Decisions
* Risk Assessments
* Capability Maturity
* Agent Performance
* Skill Performance
* Experiment Results

---

## Architectural Model

```text
Business Outcome
        ↓
Capability
        ↓
Skills
        ↓
Implementations
        ↓
Execution
        ↓
Enterprise Learning
        ↓
Collective Intelligence
        ↓
Improved Business Outcomes
```

---

## Consequences

### Positive

* Establishes a measurable enterprise AI objective
* Enables continuous capability improvement
* Reduces dependence on individual agents or models
* Promotes reuse across capabilities and solutions
* Creates an enterprise memory that improves over time
* Supports increasing levels of safe autonomy

### Tradeoffs

* Requires collection and governance of learning data
* Requires capability maturity models
* Introduces new enterprise metrics
* Requires mechanisms for measuring intelligence growth

---

## Strategic Implications

As Collective Intelligence grows:

* Recommendations become more accurate
* Execution becomes faster
* Situational awareness improves
* Strategy selection improves
* Human effort decreases
* Safe autonomy increases

The primary goal of EAIOS is not to maximize the number of agents.

The primary goal of EAIOS is to continuously improve the Collective Intelligence available to achieve enterprise business outcomes.

---

## Architectural Principle

Actions are driven by Collective Intelligence.

Collective Intelligence is built through learning.

The value of EAIOS is measured by its ability to continuously improve how effectively the enterprise achieves business outcomes.
