# ADR-005: Collective Intelligence as a First-Class Enterprise Capability

## Status

Accepted

**Date:** 2026-06-26

---

# Context

Traditional enterprise platforms manage applications, infrastructure, services, incidents, changes, and knowledge.

Many AI platforms manage models, prompts, agents, and workflows.

While these assets are valuable, they are not the primary objective of Enterprise AI.

Organizations invest in AI to improve their ability to achieve desired business outcomes.

Over time, enterprises accumulate:

* Organizational knowledge
* Operational experience
* Enterprise Memory
* Reasoning outcomes
* Governance decisions
* Human expertise
* Successful resolutions
* Failed resolutions
* Continuous learning

Together, these assets represent the organization's Collective Intelligence.

EAIOS requires an operating model capable of continuously capturing, governing, improving, and reusing this intelligence across capabilities, skills, implementations, and business outcomes.

---

# Decision

EAIOS treats Collective Intelligence as a first-class enterprise capability.

Collective Intelligence represents the enterprise's accumulated ability to achieve desired business outcomes through reusable capabilities, enterprise learning, operational experience, reasoning, governance, and continuous improvement.

Collective Intelligence belongs to the enterprise.

It is not owned by individual:

* AI Agents
* Models
* Applications
* Workflows
* Enterprise platforms

Enterprise Learning continuously enriches Collective Intelligence through Enterprise Memory.

Enterprise Reasoning transforms Collective Intelligence into explainable, governed recommendations.

---

# Components of Collective Intelligence

Collective Intelligence may include:

* Enterprise Knowledge
* Enterprise Memory
* Historical Outcomes
* Validated Patterns
* Reasoning Outcomes
* Known Errors
* Successful Resolutions
* Failed Resolutions
* Human Feedback
* Governance Decisions
* Risk Assessments
* Capability Maturity
* Skill Performance
* Implementation Performance
* Experiment Results

Enterprise Memory stores validated patterns rather than individual execution events.

# Architectural Model

```text
Desired Business Outcome
            ↓
Capability
            ↓
Skills
            ↓
Implementations
            ↓
Execution
            ↓
Enterprise Learning
            ↓
Enterprise Memory
            ↓
Collective Intelligence
            ↓
Enterprise Reasoning
            ↓
Improved Enterprise Decisions
```

---

# Consequences

## Benefits

* Establishes a measurable enterprise objective
* Enables continuous capability improvement
* Reduces dependence on individual implementations
* Promotes enterprise-wide reuse
* Creates Enterprise Memory that improves over time
* Supports progressively higher levels of safe autonomy
* Preserves organizational knowledge

---

## Trade-offs

* Requires disciplined governance of enterprise learning
* Requires Enterprise Memory lifecycle management
* Introduces new enterprise performance metrics
* Requires mechanisms for measuring organizational improvement

---

# Strategic Implications

As Collective Intelligence matures:

* Recommendations become more consistent
* Enterprise reasoning improves
* Situational awareness becomes richer
* Operational Confidence becomes more accurate
* Strategy selection becomes more adaptive
* Human effort shifts toward higher-value decisions
* Safe autonomy becomes increasingly achievable

The primary goal of EAIOS is not to maximize the number of AI agents.

The primary goal is to continuously improve the enterprise's ability to reason and achieve desired business outcomes.

---

# Architectural Principles

Learning belongs to the enterprise.

Enterprise Memory stores validated patterns.

Collective Intelligence belongs to the enterprise.

Enterprise Reasoning transforms Collective Intelligence into explainable, governed action.

The value of EAIOS is measured by continuously improving the enterprise's ability to achieve desired business outcomes.
